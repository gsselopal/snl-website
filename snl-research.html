⚡ Research Focus Areas

Pioneering Sustainable Nano-Materials

Four research pillars driving innovation at the intersection of nanotechnology, energy, and environment.

🌞

Photoelectrochemical Solar Energy

Engineering nanostructured photoelectrodes to maximize light harvesting and energy conversion.

Energy Primary Pillar

Our solar energy research focuses on engineering nanostructured photoelectrodes — primarily quantum dot-sensitized and perovskite-based systems — to maximize light harvesting efficiency and carrier lifetime.

Key Research Directions

  • Quantum dot solar cells
  • Perovskite photoelectrodes
  • Interface engineering
  • Charge transport optimization
💧

Green Hydrogen Production

Photocatalytic systems for solar-driven water splitting and clean hydrogen generation.

Hydrogen Clean Energy

We develop photocatalytic and photoelectrochemical systems for solar-driven water splitting, producing clean hydrogen without carbon emissions.

Key Research Directions

  • Photoelectrochemical water splitting
  • Cocatalyst design
  • Z-scheme systems
  • Hydrogen evolution reaction (HER)
🌿

Precision Agriculture & Nano-Sensors

Next-generation biosensors for crop monitoring, pathogen detection, and soil health.

Agriculture Sensing

Leveraging nanoparticle optical properties to build next-generation biosensors for real-time crop monitoring, pathogen detection, and soil health assessment.

Key Research Directions

  • SERS-based biosensors
  • Quantum dot biomarkers
  • Field-deployable sensor arrays
  • Soil nutrient monitoring
🧹

Environmental Remediation

Photocatalytic nanomaterials for degrading pollutants and cleaning water and air.

Environment Photocatalysis

Engineering photocatalytic nanomaterials that harness sunlight to degrade organic pollutants, heavy metals, and emerging contaminants in water and air.

Key Research Directions

  • TiO₂/ZnO photocatalysts
  • Visible-light sensitization
  • Dye degradation
  • Heavy metal removal
